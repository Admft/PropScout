Key Financial Metrics

These are the main numbers investors use to judge rental-property strength.

Cap Rate

Cap Rate estimates unlevered yield: annual NOI divided by purchase price.

General rule of thumb: under 5% is often weak, around 5–6% is thin, and 6%+ is usually more attractive, though market context matters a lot.

DSCR

DSCR means Debt Service Coverage Ratio. It tells you how comfortably NOI covers mortgage payments.

General rule of thumb: below 1.10 is stressed, 1.10–1.24 is tight, and 1.25+ is healthier.

NOI

NOI is Net Operating Income — rent collected after operating expenses, but before mortgage payments.

Higher is generally better. Negative or very thin NOI means the property may struggle even before financing is considered.

Break-even Occupancy

Break-even Occupancy is the occupancy level needed to cover operating costs plus debt service.

General rule of thumb: lower is better. Above ~90% means there is less room for vacancy or operational mistakes.
